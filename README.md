# VFIAX Mutual Fund Analysis

Comprehensive analysis of Vanguard 500 Index Fund Admiral Shares (VFIAX), evaluating performance, risk, investor suitability, and benchmark tracking against the S&P 500.

## Overview

Completed as part of **FINA 6203: Investment Analysis** at Northeastern University (Fall 2025). The project examines VFIAX's investment strategy, historical returns, risk profile, and how effectively it tracks its benchmark.

## Key Analysis Areas

- **Portfolio Objectives** — Fund strategy, IPS, investment style, and buy/sell discipline
- **Security Holdings** — Top 10 holdings breakdown and sector concentration analysis
- **Performance Analysis** — Historical returns across multiple timeframes with peer comparison
- **Risk Management** — Beta, P/E, P/B, ROE, and earnings growth metrics
- **Investor Suitability** — Ideal investor profile and risk tolerance assessment
- **Benchmark Comparison** — Tracking error analysis (<0.03%) and peer fund outperformance

## Key Findings

- VFIAX tracks the S&P 500 with a tracking error below 0.03%, underperforming by only ~0.04% annually (equal to its expense ratio)
- The fund consistently outperforms the average large blend fund by 2–3% annually, driven by its ultra-low cost structure
- Top 10 holdings represent 38.73% of the portfolio, with technology stocks dominating at 23%+
- Beta of ~1.00 confirms the fund moves in lockstep with the S&P 500
